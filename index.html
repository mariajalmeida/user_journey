Rita Sampaio

Scenario:

As a new season comes around, Rita realises the summer clothes part of her closet is lacking a bit.

Expectations:

  • Expects to find an overwhelming amount of options, yet unable to find exactly her style
  • Expects to give up and fall back into brands she already knows
  • Expects to give up shopping online, and ending up going to physical shops nearby

Stages

Doing

Thinking

Saying

Awareness

Begins by googling three different searches: Blouse, summer dress, ballerina shoes.
"How can I find an online store with my type of clothing?"
"Not really interested in feeding big brand names, yet that's all Google shows me."

Consideration

Going back and forth between big brand websites on mobile, rapidly getting overwhelmed by the amount of things.
"I don't love these, but they could be possible options if I don't find something better?"
"Maybe I can swing by the mall tomorrow and see if anything catches my eye."

Decision

Going back and forth between several Chrome tabs with a few different pieces of clothing.
"The more articles webshops seem to have, the more impossible it is to find what I want."
"I will try to find other online stores tomorrow."

Post-experience

Following smaller shops on Facebook and Instagram, as those seem to be more close to her style.
"Online shopping always takes longer than going to an actual store."
"Nothing beats the local store shopping experience."

Insights

  • For a person with a less trendy style, online shopping can be a nightmare
  • Marizé's shop could easily be the answer for someone like Rita, someone who wants quality pieces for a reasonable price

Internal ownership

  • The lack of any online presence keeps Marizé's business to a very secluded bubble
  • It is crucial to align the physical store ambient with any e-commerce solution
